Social Media Content Strategy

Stratford Karate Club + Personal Brand  ·  Goal: Fill Sunday 10am class  ·  28 Mar 2026

The Big Picture

Two accounts, two jobs:

AccountJob
Club account (Instagram / Facebook / TikTok)Drive trial sign-ups, showcase the environment
Personal (@aaron.storey.9)Build credibility — make people choose you over any other club

They feed each other. Personal account builds trust. Trust converts on the club account.

Your Unfair Advantage

Most local karate instructors can't say any of this:

  • 🥉 First male from England to medal in kata at JKA Europeans
  • 🥇 3 consecutive English national kata titles
  • 🏆 Held kata AND kumite national titles simultaneously (2023)
  • 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 10 years on the JKA England national team

This is your hook. A parent choosing between two local clubs — one run by a qualified instructor, one run by a national champion and European medallist — it's not a choice. We need this front and centre.

Club Account — Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4)

Posting frequency: 4x per week (Mon, Wed, Fri, Sun)
Format mix: 60% Reels/short video, 30% static posts, 10% stories

1. Sunday Spotlight (every Sunday)

Goal: make Sunday 10am feel unmissable. Short video of the Sunday class in action. Caption: capacity, what beginners can expect, free trial CTA.

"Sunday mornings at Stratford Karate — all ages, all abilities, 10am. We've got space. First trial is free. Link in bio."

2. Student Stories (2x per month)

Goal: social proof. Short testimonial clip or quote card. Parents talking about kids' progress work especially well. Beginners > advanced — more relatable to new sign-ups.

3. Technique/Training Clips (2x per week)

Goal: demonstrate quality. 15–30 sec drills, kata breakdowns, kumite combos. Keep it accessible. Caption with a learning angle: "This is the first thing new students learn..."

4. Behind the Scenes (1x per week)

Goal: warmth and community feel. Setting up the dojo, pre-class warmups, post-class moments. Shows the culture, not just the karate.

Personal Account (@aaron.storey.9) — Phase 1

Posting frequency: 2–3x per week  ·  Tone: Authentic, confident, not boastful.

1. The Origin Story (Week 1 — pin this)

Your story: started 2011 → national team → Europeans medal → coaching in East London. Most powerful piece of content. Pin it.

"I'm the first male from England to win a kata medal at the JKA European Championships. I teach karate in Stratford on Sunday mornings. Here's why."

2. Champion's Perspective (weekly)

Short takes on karate, competition, coaching, discipline, mindset:

  • "What I learned losing at Nationals before winning 3 in a row"
  • "The difference between a student who improves fast and one who doesn't"
  • "Why kata competition is the hardest test in karate"

3. Training Content (weekly)

Your own training, competition prep, S&C work. The PT crossover is an angle worth using.

4. Club Tie-ins (occasional)

Tag the club when relevant: "If you want to learn this, come to Sunday morning class — link in bio"
